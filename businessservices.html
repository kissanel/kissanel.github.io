CULTURES OF DISAGREEMENT

Cultures of Disagreement Diversity Training focuses on promoting Diversity of Viewpoint. This is a crucial type of diversity for the effective functioning of any organisation but is often an aspect of diversity that receives less attention within usual diversity training programmes. We live in a world with many different people, with many different perspectives and ideas. Embracing and harnessing these differences allows organisations to flourish.

Consider these questions:
  • Do your employees feel they cannot express their views?
  • Do they experience tensions in working relationships related to differing viewpoints?
  • Do they feel undervalued because they are not heard?
  • Do they avoid posing questions that may challenge the status quo?
  • Do they prioritise a sense of harmony over thought-provoking conversations?
  • Do they struggle to communicate disagreement in a productive way?

    • If this describes some of your workforce, this can result in stagnation: a stifling of ideas & creativity and a limiting of genuine connection between people. It can also negatively impact employee wellbeing and work-related stress. Diversity of viewpoint within organisations provokes critical thinking, innovation, and creative solutions that result in motivated and formidable workforces. We create Cultures of Disagreement in your organisation.

    Cultures of Disagreement Diversity Training:
  • Promotes an organisational culture that values diversity of viewpoint
  • Prompts deep reflection on specific communication styles within your organisation and teams & with the key partners with whom you work
  • Supports employees to adopt a psychological lens to analyse group processes within specific work-place contexts
  • Optimises working relationships by developing the skills of listening, understanding and productive disagreement through in-person modelling and practice
  • Deepens understanding of others’ experiences and builds connection
  • Enables your employees to take responsibility for enhancing the way in which they work to promote creativity, productivity, and wellbeing
